# CNBC Fast Money Digest - 2026-04-08

## MARKET OVERVIEW
The market saw a massive relief rally on Wednesday as the major indices ripped higher following news of a two-week ceasefire agreement between the U.S. and Iran. The Dow surged over 1,300 points (+2.85%), marking its best day in a year. Oil prices plunged toward $90 per barrel as tensions in the Middle East cooled temporarily.

## KEY INSIGHTS
- **Relief Rally**: The last-minute ceasefire agreement significantly lifted investor sentiment, which had been under extreme pressure due to threats of escalation.
- **Geopolitical Volatility**: Traders warn that while the ceasefire is a welcome reprieve, the two-week window is short, and distrust remains high (evidenced by claims of early violations).
- **Financial Sector Strength**: Financial stocks are showing technical strength heading into earnings season. Strategas' Chris Verrone noted positive technical setups in the sector.
- **CRE as Safety Play**: Easterly Government Properties CEO suggests that commercial real estate could become a key safety play in this environment.
- **Market Sentiment**: Wells Fargo's Schumacher cautioned that the market backdrop may have become "too sanguine, too quickly" following the rally.

## TRADER TAKEAWAYS

- **INFQ**: Mentioned in final trades; focus on infrastructure/tech plays.
- **Citigroup (C)**: Financials gaining momentum ahead of earnings.
