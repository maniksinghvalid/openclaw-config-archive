# CNBC Fast Money Digest — May 11, 2026 Episode

**Episode:** Consumer Crunch Intensifies… And International Opportunity 5/11/26
**Duration:** 43m
**Date Archived:** 2026-05-12

## Key Insights

1. **Retail sector cracks are widening.** The XRT retail ETF suffered its worst single-day drop in over a year (down >3%), extending a 3-week losing streak. Caleres and Kohl's each tumbled more than 9%, signaling consumers pulling back on discretionary spending ahead of retail earnings week.

2. **S&P 500 closed above 7,400 for the first time** at 7,412.84, driven entirely by Big Tech. The Nasdaq also hit a fresh record at 26,274 while the Dow reached 49,704. The panel noted the divergence: consumers struggling while tech boom powers the broader market.

3. **Copper hit an all-time closing high of $6.4605/lb**, surging >2% intraday, now up >13% in 2026. Discussed as a beneficiary of AI infrastructure buildout and supply constraints.

4. **Netflix shares got "chilled"** as the ad-free standard plan reached $20/month. Panel debated whether streaming economics are approaching a tipping point where ad-supported tiers become more profitable.

5. **Aehr Test Systems (AEHR) CEO joined the show** — the semi test/validation company has quintupled over the last year. Surging AI chip testing demand driving orders ahead of expectations.

6. **Iran war overhang continues, markets tuning it out.** Oil surged 2.8% (WTI $98, Brent $104) after Trump rejected Iran's latest proposal. Wolfe Research argued return to hot war is unlikely.

7. **Citi made Nasdaq 100 the preferred AI buildout play.** PEG ratio near two-decade lows suggests attractive growth for the premium paid, but expectations leave little room for error.
