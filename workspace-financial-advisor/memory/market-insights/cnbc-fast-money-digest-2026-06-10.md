*CNBC's "Fast Money"*
🎧 *A Decline in Chip Stocks Becomes Market Sell-off… Where Tech Sector is Heading*

_Melissa Lee and the Fast Money roundtable break down a volatile session where chip stocks reversed sharply amid escalating US-Iran tensions — plus Kalshi CEO Tarek Mansour on the explosive launch of regulated perpetual futures._

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
💡 *KEY INSIGHTS*
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

• *Chip stocks suffered a violent intraday reversal* — The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index (SOXX) swung from +3% to −8.6% in hours after news broke that Iran shot down a U.S. military helicopter, erasing Monday's 6% relief rally in the SMH semiconductor ETF. Micron and Broadcom gave back all of their Monday gains.

• *Geopolitical risk has overtaken AI enthusiasm as the dominant market driver* — Trump's Truth Social post that the US "must react" triggered an instant sell-off across tech, while the Strait of Hormuz disruption (20% of global oil transit) is now the single biggest variable for inflation and Fed policy. The May CPI print on Wednesday is the next flashpoint.

• *Defensives are in, tech is out — a five-session rotation* — Nine of 11 S&P 500 sectors closed green on Tuesday, but only technology and energy finished in the red. Consumer staples, healthcare, utilities, and financials are absorbing capital flowing out of mega-cap growth, signaling a regime shift toward risk-off positioning.

• *Kalshi hit $1 billion in perpetual futures volume in its first week* — CEO Tarek Mansour revealed that Kalshi's waitlist for perps exceeded 1 million people, making it the fastest-growing product in company history. The CFTC-approved contracts are the first-ever regulated perp market in the US, opening a $90 trillion annual global market to domestic traders. Kalshi took 40 months to reach $1B in event contracts — perps did it in 7 days.

• *Apple stock is sliding after a disappointing WWDC* — Despite hitting an intraday all-time high, AAPL reversed sharply as investors concluded the Siri AI announcements lacked the "wow factor" needed to justify its premium valuation. The sell-off reflects a broader malaise: the market is punishing companies that promise AI but fail to deliver a clear product-market fit.

• *The Dow and Russell 2000 outperformed the Nasdaq on Tuesday* — The Dow rose 86 points (+0.17%) and small-caps eked out a gain, while the Nasdaq Composite dropped −0.97%. This divergence confirms money is rotating out of crowded large-cap tech trades into value, cyclicals, and defensives — classic late-cycle behavior.

• *VIX remains elevated at ~20-22, and the 10-year yield sits at ~4.56%* — Neither VIX nor yields have normalized despite the partial recovery into Tuesday's close, suggesting traders are pricing in sustained uncertainty around both geopolitics and the inflation outlook.
