# CNBC Fast Money Digest — June 3, 2026 (Episode of 6/3)
*Episode: "A Sign Of Market Euphoria?... And Opportunity In Metals"*

## Overview
The Fast Money panel debates AI/semi stock euphoria, SpaceX's record-shattering IPO, CrowdStrike earnings, credit card stock weakness, and metals as a portfolio hedge. CME CEO Terry Duffy joins to warn on perpetual futures.

## Key Insights

1. **Market Euphoria Watch** — Chip and AI stocks hit fresh records with semiconductors surging ~80% in 2026. The panel debates whether the rally is justified by AI demand or flashing froth. STMicroelectronics, Marvell, and HPE leading; Intel up nearly 200%.

2. **SpaceX's Record-Setting IPO** — SpaceX plans to raise $75B at $135/share, valuing the company at ~$1.77 trillion — the largest IPO in history. The panel weighs implications for the broader market.

3. **CME's Terry Duffy Warns on Perpetual Futures** — Duffy argues perpetual futures "incite bad behavior" and are primarily retail-oriented speculation tools, not hedging instruments. He warns they could be dangerous for retail investors and may violate U.S. law. Exchange stocks (Cboe, ICE) sold off on approval fears.

4. **CrowdStrike Beats but Sells Off 9%** — CRWD Q1 FY2027: EPS $1.10 (vs $1.07 est), revenue $1.39B (+26% YoY). Record net new ARR of $255.8M (+32% YoY). But billings of $1.35B (+17.7% YoY) missed estimates. CEO Kurtz called it "the Mythos moment" — AI security as critical infrastructure. 4-for-1 stock split announced.

5. **Credit Card Stocks Swipe Lower** — Visa, Mastercard, American Express declined. Continued underperformance in payments space post-Q1 earnings. Macro fears around tariffs and consumer spending headwinds resetting valuations.

6. **Opportunity in Metals** — Panel explores metals as diversification amid AI-driven equity concentration risk. Gold anchoring precious metals basket; mining equities and metals ETFs discussed as hedge against equity euphoria and inflation persistence.
