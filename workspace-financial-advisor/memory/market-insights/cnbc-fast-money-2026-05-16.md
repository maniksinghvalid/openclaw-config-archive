# CNBC Fast Money — May 15, 2026

**Episode:** Stocks Drop, Yields Rise… And Retail Picks Ahead Of Results

## KEY INSIGHTS

1. **Broad sell-off across asset classes** — Stocks, bonds, gold, and silver all slumped on Friday as mounting inflation fears gripped markets. The Dow fell 537 points (-1.07%), S&P 500 lost 1.24%, and the Nasdaq dropped 1.54%. The 10-year Treasury yield hit 4.544% — its highest in nearly a year — while the 30-year topped 5.1%.

2. **Tech profit-taking hits semiconductors hard** — Semi stocks led the decline after an unsustainable run. Intel dropped 6%+, AMD -5.7%, Micron -6.6%, Nvidia -4.4%. Cerebras Systems gave back 10% after its 68% IPO pop on Thursday. Analysts called the move "extremely unsustainable" and vulnerable to profit-taking regardless of headlines.

3. **Microsoft bucks the trend on Ackman buy-in** — MSFT rose 3% after Bill Ackman revealed Pershing Square built a position in Q1. The stock was the notable exception in an otherwise brutal tech session.

4. **Oil surge past $105 threatens recession** — WTI crude settled at $105.42 (+4.2%), Brent at $109.26. Dan Niles warned on CNBC that 10 of the last 12 recessions were preceded by oil spikes of this magnitude. With oil up ~50% since the Iran conflict escalated, he cautioned a sustained spike of a quarter or two could trigger a downturn.

5. **Fed rate hike now on the table** — For the first time this cycle, fed funds futures are pricing in a rate HIKE as the Fed's next move. December hike probability: ~51%, January: ~60%, March 2027: >71%. This follows a week of hot inflation data, with incoming Chair Kevin Warsh facing a hawkish backdrop.

6. **Trump-Xi summit underwhelms markets** — The Trump/Xi summit concluded with no major policy breakthroughs. The only notable deal — 200 Boeing jets — was seen as only marginally above expectations. Boeing fell 3.8% on Friday, extending losses. The Strait of Hormuz "must remain open" was a headline but lacked enforcement details.

7. **Resource rout hits metals and miners** — Gold fell 2% to $4,552; silver cratered 7.7%. Silver miners and ETFs were hit hard: ProShares Ultra Silver ETF -12%, Teck Resources -5.9%. A stronger dollar (+0.4%) and "higher-for-longer" rate expectations drove the commodity sell-off.

8. **Retail earnings loom as consumer health test** — Next week brings major retail earnings: Home Depot, Target, Walmart, TJX, Ralph Lauren, and VF Corp. The Fast Money panel discussed which names a top analyst is recommending ahead of results, as the market looks for a read on the US consumer amid rising rates and inflation.
