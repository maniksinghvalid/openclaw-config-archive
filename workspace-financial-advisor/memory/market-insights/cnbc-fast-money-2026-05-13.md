# CNBC Fast Money Digest — May 13, 2026
> Episode: May 12, 2026 — "Rates Spike, Stocks Pull Back On Rising Inflation… And CEOs Joining Trump's China Trip"

## KEY INSIGHTS

1. **April CPI came in at 3.8% YoY** (above 3.7% forecast and up sharply from March's 3.3%), while core CPI hit 2.8% — the highest since September 2025 — sending the S&P 500 and Nasdaq into a pullback from record highs.

2. **Energy prices were the primary inflation driver**, surging 17.9% annually with gasoline up 28.4%, directly tied to the ongoing US-Iran conflict pushing oil above $103/barrel. Energy accounted for more than 40% of the headline CPI gain.

3. **Inflation is broadening beyond energy** — shelter costs reaccelerated to +0.6% monthly and food-at-home prices saw their biggest monthly gain since August 2022, making it harder for the Fed to look through the energy-driven spike.

4. **Tech shares led the declines** with the Nasdaq falling 0.9%. Qualcomm plunged 13% in its worst session since 2020, pulling the semiconductor sector back from a massive AI-fueled rally that had seen the SOX up 60% YTD.

5. **The hot CPI data effectively takes a June rate cut off the table**, with the panel debating whether the Fed's next move could even be a hike if inflation continues accelerating — a sharp shift from earlier dovish expectations.

6. **GameStop CEO Ryan Cohen's $56B unsolicited bid for eBay** was formally rejected by eBay's board, which called it "neither credible nor attractive" — raising questions about financing and Cohen's strategic vision for the meme-stock darling turned would-be acquirer.

7. **The Chartmaster highlighted a gold miner** that has seen a massive multi-year run, suggesting profit-taking may be prudent given gold's pullback from $4,704 toward $4,678 amid rising real yields on Treasury bonds.

8. **CEOs reportedly joining Trump on a China trip** amid escalating trade tensions — the panel views this as a positive signal for US-China negotiations despite broader geopolitical uncertainty from the Iran conflict.
