# CNBC Fast Money — May 20, 2026 Episode

**Title:** Nvidia Reports Results… And Stocks Bounce Back As Rate Surge Cools Off
**Date:** May 20, 2026
**Duration:** 44m

---

## Key Insights

1. **Nvidia crushed estimates but barely budged** — Q1 FY2027 revenue hit a record ~$82B (data center nearly doubled YoY). Despite the beat, NVDA slipped ~1% after-hours because expectations were already priced in. Fast Money's Dan Nathan had called this exact scenario on Tuesday: expectations so elevated that good news can't move the stock higher.

2. **The Fed minutes leaned hawkish** — A majority of FOMC participants flagged that the Middle East conflict could keep exacerbating inflation, meaning rate *hikes* remain on the table for later this year. The probability of no cut through December dropped sharply. A top market strategist joined the traders — the takeaway: the bar to hike is still high, but the bar to cut is now even higher.

3. **Crude's 5%+ plunge was the day's biggest catalyst** — A sharp drop in oil prices (driven by Iran peace deal hopes) fueled the broader rally. The Dow surged 650+ points, and airlines and travel stocks were the biggest beneficiaries. The panel noted this one-day oil move could unwind quickly if talks stall.

4. **TJX showed consumers are trading down in force** — TJX crushed estimates ($1.19 EPS vs $1.02 expected) and raised full-year guidance. Shares jumped 6%+, proving budget-conscious shoppers are flocking to off-price. The panel called this the clearest signal that the "trade-down trade" is alive and well.

5. **Target disappointed and told a different consumer story** — Unlike TJX, Target gave weak guidance and its stock fell. The divergence between TJX (value-seeking) and Target (discretionary) shows the consumer is bifurcating: still spending, but only at the cheapest price points.

6. **Bond yields finally retreated after spooking markets all week** — The 10-year yield pulled back sharply, giving stocks room to bounce. Treasuries had been in the "danger zone" per strategists. The panel debated whether this is a genuine reversal or just a pause before yields push higher.

7. **Nvidia authorized an $80B buyback** — As part of its earnings, NVDA's board authorized a massive $80B share repurchase program. The traders viewed this as a signal management sees the stock as undervalued even here, though muted after-market reaction says the market needs to see actual execution.
