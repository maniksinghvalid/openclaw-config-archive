# CNBC Fast Money Digest - April 14, 2026

## MARKET OVERVIEW
The major indices saw a strong relief rally on Tuesday, with the Nasdaq leading the way, gaining nearly 2%. The rally was primarily driven by optimism surrounding a potential ceasefire deal with Iran and a significant bounce-back in the software sector, which had been heavily beaten down.

## KEY THEMES
- **Middle East Optimism:** Hopes for a lasting ceasefire between the US and Iran pushed oil prices lower and boosted risk appetite across the equity markets.
- **Software Sector Recovery:** After a period of underperformance, software stocks saw a massive rotation back into the sector, led by major cloud and AI infrastructure plays.
- **Data Center Power Race:** The partnership expansion between Bloom Energy (BE) and Oracle (ORCL) to deploy up to 2.8 gigawatts of fuel cell capacity highlighted the critical "time-to-power" bottleneck in AI scaling.

## TRADER COMMENTARY
- **Tech Rally:** The traders discussed the resilience of the tech sector, noting that mega-cap and enterprise software names are becoming "must-own" infrastructure plays as AI deployment accelerates.
- **Oracle / Bloom Energy:** The panel highlighted Bloom Energy's surge (+23.98%) as a sign that infrastructure and power delivery are now the primary currencies for AI growth.

## FINAL TRADES
- **Guy Adami:** **Toyota (TM)** — Betting on a broader industrial recovery and currency tailwinds.
- **Tim Seymour:** **Intel (INTC)** — Playing the continued rebound in semiconductor and AI infrastructure demand.

## KEY INSIGHTS
- Software stocks are seeing a fundamental rethinking as critical AI infrastructure, leading to a massive sector rotation.
- "Time-to-power" is now the most important metric for data center scaling, as evidenced by the Bloom Energy / Oracle deal.
- Geopolitical relief (Iran ceasefire) is acting as a primary catalyst for the current market move, but sustainability remains a point of debate.
