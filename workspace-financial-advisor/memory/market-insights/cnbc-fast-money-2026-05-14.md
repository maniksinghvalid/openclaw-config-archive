## KEY INSIGHTS

1. **Jensen Huang joins Trump on Air Force One to China** — After CNBC initially reported Huang was not invited, Trump personally called the Nvidia CEO and asked him to join the delegation, flying him to Alaska to board Air Force One. The debate shifted to what this means for US-China chip policy and Nvidia's exposure to Beijing.

2. **Kevin Warsh confirmed as Fed Chair in 54-45 vote** — The Senate confirmed Warsh on Wednesday, handing Trump his preferred candidate amid rising inflation (CPI 3.8%) and pressure to lower rates from the current 3.5-3.75% range. Warsh inherits a Fed facing the most complex inflation environment since the early 1980s.

3. **Cisco Q3 beats and raises for third straight quarter** — Revenue of $15.8B and non-GAAP EPS of $1.06 both topped consensus. Full-year guidance raised to $62.8-63.0B (vs $61.6B consensus). Cisco's AI networking story is finally resonating with Wall Street after the stock hit records in 2026.

4. **Ford surged 13% to lead the S&P 500** — Shares exploded after Morgan Stanley upgraded the stock to Buy with a $175 target, citing a "fairly high likelihood" Ford signs a large-scale Energy Storage Systems supply agreement with a large commercial customer.

5. **Chartmaster sells semiconductors into strength** — With the SOX index up 60% YTD and semi stocks at all-time highs, the Chartmaster segment advised taking profits. The panel discussed whether the semi trade has become too crowded.

6. **S&P 500 and Nasdaq close at records** — The S&P 500 edged up 0.18% to 7,414 and the Nasdaq hit new highs, driven by tech despite a majority of stocks closing lower. The market is looking through inflation concerns and geopolitical risks.

7. **Semiconductor breadth widening** — ON Semiconductor (+7.9%) and Akamai (+5.5%) also rallied, indicating the AI infrastructure trade is broadening beyond the Magnificent Seven into networking and specialty semi names.
